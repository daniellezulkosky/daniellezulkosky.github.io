RxR Marathon

By Danielle Zulkosky and Hannah Loesch

The 2018 Marathon Weekend took place on May 5th and 6th.

On Saturday there was a Kids Fun Run, a One Mile Run and a 5K Run.

Sunday there was a 10K Run, Half Marathon and a Full Marathon.

“I’ve been running different races for probably the last six or seven years. I enjoy coming out to Nassau County races because I get to run through my neighborhood, and see my friends along the way. It feels good to have that support out there” said Monica Jewidowicz of Nassau County.

“For track runners it’s a great experience to watch all these people train all their lives to run a marathon, and then actually do it. Also, it’s pretty cool watching them run up to you and to be able to give them some water,” said Daniela Vazquez, a track and field runner at Great Neck North High School, pictured with her coach before handing out water.

“I actually ran today for the Thomas Matthew Miloscia Foundation. He’s our friend who passed away in 2015 from a rare form of cancer. I ran it in support of him. It was really emotional. Every time that I wanted to give up, I’d just think of Tom and keep going,” said Keira McIsaac of Central Islip.

Runners started the race at Nassau Community College and ran to Eisenhower Park.

Thomas Rammelkamp, 28, formerly of Miller Place who now lives in Virginia, was the overall winner of the marathon with a time of 2 hours, 33 minutes, and 12 seconds.

Gabrielle Russo, 33, of Patchogue, won the women's division with an unofficial time of two hours, 50 minutes, 36 seconds, according to Newsday.
